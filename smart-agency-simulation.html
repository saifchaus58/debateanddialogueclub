About the Event

The Smart Agency Simulation is an interactive strategic investigation event where participants step into the role of intelligence agencies to analyze complex cybersecurity scenarios. Through case studies, hidden evidence, misleading information, and unexpected twists, teams must investigate threats, form strategies, and defend their conclusions in a high-pressure deliberation environment.

Participants will work in teams to examine a complex case study involving fake news, misleading evidence, encrypted messages, suspicious data, and conflicting narratives. Teams must carefully analyze available information, identify hidden loopholes, research the background of the issue, and develop well-reasoned arguments supported by evidence.

The event replicates the pressure and complexity faced by intelligence agencies and policy strategists when dealing with cybersecurity threats. Midway through, a sudden crisis twist is introduced, forcing participants to reassess their analysis and adapt under pressure.

Simulation Phases

Phase 1: Intelligence Briefing

Receive a detailed case file containing intercepted emails, encrypted codes, news headlines, and suspicious data charts. Decode hidden messages and identify potential threats.

Phase 2: Investigation & Research

Examine the case study in depth, identify loopholes, and research background. Prepare a working paper summarizing analysis and initial conclusions.

Phase 3: Closed-Door Strategy Session

Internal deliberation to decide strategic stance. Identify responsibilities, evaluate actions, and decide on beneficial alliances.

Phase 4: International Security Council Deliberation

Present findings before a jury panel. Engage in high-level debate, questioning, and counter-arguments with other intelligence units.

Phase 5: Crisis Twist & Final Adjournment

A new revelation changes everything. Reassess strategy instantly, revise conclusions, and present final findings under time pressure.
